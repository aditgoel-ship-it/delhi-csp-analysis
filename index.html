Bottom 150 — what this adds

Exit rate (23%) is 4× the rest 653. The 42% uncoded block — CSPs who gave no reason at all — is itself a disengagement signal. But disgruntled and mistrust rates are the same across both groups, so commercial frustration is not unique to the failing CSPs. The PNM data confirms the trajectory; it doesn't add a new diagnosis.

Rest 653 — what this adds

The service numbers look fine, but 34% of coded denials here are commercial grievances from currently-performing CSPs. This is not in the service metrics. 11 large CSPs (Bhatia 550, AVI 314, MGN 404) weren't real denials — just missed visits. The commercial trust signal is worth watching separately from service quality.

CSP App Rollout

As we roll the CSP app — track
the two groups separately.

If we measure Delhi as one number, these 7,905 customers will keep dragging every metric — and make the app's impact on the other 83,249 invisible.

Measuring Delhi whole

The failing 7,905 customers suppress every aggregate. TAT looks worse than it is. Renewal looks worse. Progress is unreadable. We risk calling the app's rollout ineffective when it's working for 91% of the base.

Tracking separately

83,249 customers give us the clean signal for what the app is doing. The 7,905 sit on a separate intervention track. The 63 reachable CSPs (bad SLA, app open) can be reached directly through push notifications — the app is their intervention channel too.
