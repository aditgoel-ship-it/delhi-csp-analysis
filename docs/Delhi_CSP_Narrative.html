The two failure modes point to different interventions. CSPs failing at Stage 1 — not marking interest — are disengaged or disintermediating. CSP App Rollout

As we roll the CSP app — track
the two groups separately.

If we measure Delhi as one number, these 7,905 customers will keep dragging every metric — and make the app's impact on the other 83,249 invisible.

Measuring Delhi whole

The failing 7,905 customers suppress every aggregate. TAT looks worse than it is. Renewal looks worse. Progress is unreadable. We risk calling the app's rollout ineffective when it's working for 91% of the base.

Tracking separately

83,249 customers give us the clean signal for what the app is doing. The 7,905 sit on a separate intervention track. The 63 reachable CSPs (bad SLA, app open) can be reached directly through push notifications — the app is their intervention channel too.

Install ratio is 5–10pp lower at every percentile. Only 22 of 150 pass. This is the foundational capability — if a CSP can't convert leads to installs, the other metrics can't improve. Low PNM penetration (54% vs 72%) suggests limited visibility into why.

What this is and isn't: This is where the capability reset effort should be sequenced — resolution first, complaint handling second, install execution third. The S Score data confirms this: 47% of the 150 score Below 88 on S Score (service quality) vs 28% of rest, a 19pp gap. T Score barely differentiates (55% Excellent for worst vs 51% rest), meaning churn risk is distributed — the 150 have a service execution problem, not uniquely a customer retention problem. The intervention is operational, not commercial.
